Resources

More resources to come but here are a few that you can indulge in for now:

  • Khan Academy

    • A renowned educational platform offering a wide range of free online courses, video lessons, and interactive exercises. Khan Academy covers subjects from math and science to humanities, making it an invaluable resource for first-generation and low-income students seeking to enhance their academic skills and knowledge.

  • Crash Course

    • A YouTube channel known for its engaging and informative video series on various subjects, Crash Course provides concise lessons and overviews that are perfect for visual learners. With topics spanning science, history, literature, and more, it's a great supplement to traditional classroom learning that is accessible to all students.

  • Ted-Ed

    • A platform that brings TED's mission of spreading ideas to education. TED-Ed features short animated lessons, created in collaboration with educators and animators, covering a wide range of topics. These engaging lessons encourage critical thinking and exploration, making learning enjoyable for students of all backgrounds.

  • Veritasium

    • A YouTube channel hosted by Derek Muller, a science communicator, educator, and filmmaker. Veritasium explores fascinating scientific concepts through engaging videos that challenge common misconceptions and inspire curiosity. The channel's approachable content helps students gain a deeper understanding of the world around them.

  • QuestBridge

    • A platform that connects high-achieving low-income students with opportunities for college scholarships and educational resources.

  • Federal Student Aid

    • The U.S. Department of Education's official resource for federal student aid programs, including grants, loans, and work-study opportunities.

  • I'm First

    • A resource dedicated to first-generation college students, providing information about colleges, scholarships, advice, and support.

  • Scholarships.com

    • A scholarship search platform that allows students to find various scholarships based on their background, interests, and academic achievements.

  • College Greenlight

    • A platform that helps first-generation and underrepresented students find colleges, scholarships, and other opportunities.

  • Coursera

    • An online learning platform that partners with universities and institutions worldwide to offer a wide range of courses, specializations, and even full degrees online.

  • MIT OpenCourseWare

    • Massachusetts Institute of Technology's initiative to provide free access to a vast collection of course materials, lectures, and resources from various disciplines.

  • edX

    • A platform offering online courses and degrees in collaboration with universities and institutions globally. edX provides a wide variety of subjects, allowing first-generation and low-income students to access high-quality education from renowned institutions, often with the option of earning verified certificates or even degrees.

  • OpenStax

    • A nonprofit organization that produces free, peer-reviewed, openly licensed textbooks for college and high school courses. OpenStax aims to reduce the financial burden on students by providing free, high-quality educational materials that can be accessed by anyone, enhancing accessibility to education.

  • Scholars Online

    • A community-driven platform that offers free resources, including textbooks, study guides, and practice exams, aimed at supporting students in various academic subjects. Scholars Online provides a collaborative environment for learners to access shared knowledge and excel in their studies.
